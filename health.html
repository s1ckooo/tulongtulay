Non-Profit Health Organization


A non-profit health organization is an entity that operates for the public benefit, with the primary goal of promoting and improving health outcomes rather than generating profits for private individuals or shareholders. These organizations focus on addressing health issues, providing healthcare services, conducting research, and implementing programs to enhance the well-being of communities. Non-profit healthcare organizations were established for charitable purposes, often by religious orders. As tax-exempt organizations, non-profit healthcare providers have a mission of serving communities and providing care without regard to the patient's ability to pay. These organizations may offer a variety of programs and services, such as health screenings, educational workshops, vaccination clinics, and support groups. Some non-profits also operate healthcare facilities or partner with existing healthcare providers.


Non-profit health organizations may conduct research to advance medical knowledge, develop new treatments, or contribute to the understanding of specific health conditions. Many non-profit health organizations engage in advocacy efforts to influence public policy and promote laws and regulations that support their mission and the well-being of the community. Non-profit organizations are generally expected to maintain a high level of transparency and accountability. They often publish financial reports, share information about their activities, and adhere to ethical standards. Examples of non-profit health organizations include the Philippine Red Cross, and the World Health Organization (WHO). These organizations play a crucial role in addressing global health challenges and improving the well-being of individuals and communities.

Philippine Mental Health Association


Philippine Mental Health Associatio is a private, non-stock, non-profit organization that provides premier Mental Health Services through Education, Advocacy, Intervention and Research. It dedicated to the promotion of mental health and prevention of mental disorders. PMHA exists to promote mental health and well-being among the general population, prevent mental disorders, eradicate stigma, and inspire individuals to become mental health advocates. PMHA is recognized by the DOH as one of the leading stakeholders in the Philippine mental health system.


Health Futures Foundation Philippines


Health Futures Foundation, Inc. (HFI) is a non-stock, non-profit, non-government organization involved in the management of health and social development programs in marginalized communities in the Philippines. They envision that all Filipino communities and families achieve and sustain optimum health and development. They empower local governments and marginalized communities in achieving access, quality, and equity in health development by creating communities of wellness.


Roots of Health


Roots of Health (Ugat ng Kalusugan) is a nonprofit organization focused on improving the reproductive, maternal and sexual health of women, girls, and their communities in Puerto Princesa, Palawan in the Philippines. Roots of Health is committed to working towards a vision of the Philippines where everyone is empowered to access sexual and reproductive health services free from stigma and discrimination. The organization's three-pronged strategy focuses on clinical services, education, and system strengthening.



Philippine Red Cross


The Philippine Red Cross is a non-profit humanitarian organization and a member of the International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement. Born officially in 1947, but with roots that traces back to the revolutionary days, the Philippine Red Cross has truly become the premier humanitarian organization in the country, committed to provide quality life-saving services that protect the life and dignity especially of indigent Filipinos in vulnerable situations. Their primary mission is to alleviate human suffering, promote health and safety, and enhance the quality of life.


Cancer Warriors Foundation


Cancer Warriors Foundation is a Philippine based parent organization of children with childhood cancer, leading the fight for children/adolescents, survivors and their families. It provides financial assistance for early diagnosis, timely treatment and appropriate follow up care. It engages and empowers parents, caregivers and support networks of the kids through education and learning sessions integrated into monthly family meetings. They ADVOCATE, so that there will be available, affordable, good quality childhood cancer drugs and medication.


Philippine General Hospital Medical Foundation.


The Philippine General Hospital Medical Foundation, Inc. (PGHMFI) was established as a non-stock, non-profit private organization that was conceived to assist the Philippine General Hospital (PGH) in achieving its noble mission of providing excellent health care for its indigent patients. The foundation’s programs focused on the following: (1) The Charity Patients Medical Fund; (2) Provision of equipment and Supplies; (3) Infrastructure Development; and (4) Research and Training.
