Non-Profit Environmental Organization


A non-profit environmentalal organization is a type of organization that operates for the primary purpose of promoting and advocating for environmental conservation, sustainability, and protection. Unlike for-profit businesses, non-profit organizations are not driven by profit motives but rather by a mission to address social or environmental issues. These organizations typically rely on donations, grants, and membership fees to fund their activities. The funds raised are used to support various initiatives and projects aimed at preserving and improving the environment. Non-profit environmental organizations often engage in activities such as advocacy, research, education, and on-the-ground conservation efforts.


These organizations may focus on a range of environmental issues, including biodiversity conservation, climate change mitigation, pollution prevention, sustainable development, and environmental education. Their work can involve lobbying for policy changes, conducting scientific research, raising public awareness, and implementing conservation projects on local, national, or global scales. These activities collectively contribute to the overarching goal of non-profit environmental organizations, which is to protect and preserve the environment for current and future generations.

Oceana Philippines


Oceana, founded in 2001, is the largest international advocacy organization focused solely on ocean conservation. Part of the global Oceana organization, Oceana Philippines works to protect and restore the oceans through science-based campaigns focused on fisheries and marine life conservation. Oceana Philippines seeks to restore the abundance of the Philippine seas. They focus on addressing issues such as overfishing, habitat destruction, pollution, and the overall health of the oceans. They often engage in advocacy, scientific research, and policy work to promote sustainable fisheries management and protect marine ecosystems.


Haribon Foundation


HARIBON® Foundation for the Conservation of Natural Resources, Inc. ("HARIBON") has been at the forefront of protecting and restoring the country’s diverse ecosystems and communities for 50 years and counting. Dedicated to the conservation of biodiversity and the sustainable use of natural resources through community-based initiatives, education, and advocacy. The Haribon Foundation logo highlights the Haring Ibon or the King of Birds; just like the first and original logo of the Haribon society in 1972. Haring Ibon is the perfect barometer to the health of our natural ecosystems, so upon closer inspection, various features reveal themselves.


Philippine Rural Reconstruction Movement


PRRM envisions a world of equity and sustainability. Focused on sustainable development, PRRM works on projects related to natural resource management, climate change adaptation, and community resilience. PRRM envisions a world of equity and sustainability. The future is one where society is free of ignorance, poverty, disease, and powerlessness; and development takes place within the environment’s carrying capacity. PRRM has made a number of major contributions to Philippine rural development. It pioneered a whole era of rural development and local democracy in the country.



Save Philippine Seas


The Philippines has been described as the center of marine biodiversity — but also the center of marine adversity. Save Philippine Seas (SPS) is a non-profit organization that aims to conserve coastal and marine resources by empowering seatizens for collective action and behavior change. Advocating for the protection and conservation of marine resources, as well as raising awareness about marine biodiversity and sustainable practices. Save Philippine Seas (SPS) is an independent movement that aims to protect the country's rich but threatened marine resources through IEC campaigns and community-based projects.


Forest Foundation Philippines


Forest Foundation Philippines, which has been around since 2002, is an organization that provides several grants to other organizations and individuals that work to protect our large hectares of greenery. Forest Foundation Philippines primarily supports projects that protect and conserve the country’s most critical forest landscapes: Sierra Madre, Palawan, Samar and Leyte, and Mindanao.Its main goal is the protection and sustainable management of Philippine forests and its biodiversity, which will be accomplished through four main objectives: Grow forests, grow livelihoods, grow partnerships, and grow advocates.


Philippine Native Plants Conservation Society, Inc.


The Philippine Native Plants Conservation Society, Inc. (abbreviated PNPCSI), is a non-profit organization which is located in Quezon City, Philippines. The society was founded in 2007. The society is devoted to the conservation of indigenous Philippine plants and their natural habitats. The society is made up of people from many walks of life which include academics, government agencies, non-government agencies, plant enthusiasts, and garden clubs and horticulture groups. PNPCSI members advocate for the appreciation, study, and conservation of all Philippine native plant.
